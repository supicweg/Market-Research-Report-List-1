<p><ul><li>Pressurized Water Reactor (PWR)</li><li>Boiling Water Reactor (BWR)</li><li>Pressurized Heavy Water Reactor (PHWR)</li><li>High Temperature Gas-Cooled Reactor (HTGR)</li><li>Others</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The nuclear power plant and equipment market comprises several reactor types, each with unique characteristics. Pressurized Water Reactors (PWR) use water under pressure to cool and moderate the reactor, while Boiling Water Reactors (BWR) allow water to boil directly in the reactor core, generating steam. Pressurized Heavy Water Reactors (PHWR) use heavy water as a moderator and coolant. High Temperature Gas-Cooled Reactors (HTGR) utilize gas as a coolant and operate at high temperatures, enhancing efficiency. Other reactor types include advanced and experimental designs.</p></p>
