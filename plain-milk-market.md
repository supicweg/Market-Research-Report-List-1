<p><p>The plain milk market is categorized into three primary types: Raw Fresh Milk, Pasteurized Milk, and Normal Temperature Milk. Raw Fresh Milk is unprocessed and requires refrigeration, offering natural nutrients but posing safety risks. Pasteurized Milk undergoes heat treatment to kill harmful bacteria, making it safer for consumption while preserving nutritional content. Normal Temperature Milk, often ultra-pasteurized, has a longer shelf life and can be stored without refrigeration until opened, appealing to consumers seeking convenience and extended usability.</p></p>
