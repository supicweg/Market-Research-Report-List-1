<p><p>The eye tracking hardware market is predominantly divided into two types: screen eye trackers and wearable eye trackers. Screen eye trackers are devices mounted on or near computer screens, capturing eye movements to analyze user interaction with digital content. Conversely, wearable eye trackers are portable devices, often integrated into glasses, allowing for tracking in natural environments. Both types serve diverse applications, from user experience research to psychological studies, enabling a deeper understanding of visual attention and behavior in various contexts.</p></p>
