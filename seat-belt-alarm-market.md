<p><p>The seat belt alarm market is categorized into two main types: Wireless Alarm Systems and Wired Alarm Systems. Wireless Alarm Systems use radio frequency signals to detect whether a seat belt is fastened, providing flexibility in installation and reducing wiring complexities. In contrast, Wired Alarm Systems are directly connected to the vehicle's electrical system, ensuring reliable communication and often lower maintenance needs. Both systems aim to enhance passenger safety by alerting occupants to fasten their seat belts, thus encouraging safer driving habits.</p></p>
