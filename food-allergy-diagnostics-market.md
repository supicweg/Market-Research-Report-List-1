<p><ul><li>Polymerase Chain reaction (PCR)</li><li>Enzyme-linked Immunosorbent Assay (ELISA)</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The food allergy diagnostics market primarily includes two types: Polymerase Chain Reaction (PCR) and Enzyme-linked Immunosorbent Assay (ELISA). PCR is a molecular technique used to detect specific allergens by amplifying their genetic material, allowing for precise identification. In contrast, ELISA is an immunological method that quantifies allergen-specific antibodies in a sample, helping to determine sensitivity levels. Both methods play crucial roles in accurate allergy diagnosis and management, catering to the growing demand for reliable testing solutions in the healthcare sector.</p></p>
